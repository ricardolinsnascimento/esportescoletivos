Handball passes

Portuguese version

Exist 5 types of passes in handball:

  • Shoulder pass
  • Pass in pronation
  • Pass behind the body
  • Behind-the-head pass
  • Bounced pass

Shoulder pass

What is a shoulder pass?

The shoulder pass is performed in a straight or parabolic trajectory. And it is done with the same movement as the pitch, but with less force. In this pass, the arm that will perform the pass must be flexed, and the palm of the hand pointing in the direction of the pass with the fingers up.

Pass in pronation

What is a pass in pronation?

The pass in pronation is performed by holding the ball with the palm facing down and performing an outward turn of the wrist. In the forward pass, the elbow should be slightly bent and the torso slightly bent forward.

Pass behind the body

What is a pass behind the body?

The pass behind the body is the pass made at the height of the head with extension of the wrist throwing the ball backwards. It is performed by throwing the ball behind the body, it can be done in a lateral or diagonal direction.

Behind-the-head pass

What is a behind-the-head pass?

The behind-the-head pass in handball can be executed either diagonally or laterally. For this pass to be accurate, the torso must be slightly inclined forward, with a slight inclination of the same to the opposite side of the executing arm, which will be projected behind the body.

Bounced pass

What is a bounced pass?

The bounced pass is performed when the ball touches the ground once before being received by the teammate, in this type of pass the ball is thrown to the ground in a diagonal trajectory.

What is the difference between passing and pitching?

Pass:It is about sending and directing the ball to a teammate, the reception must be made with one or two hands, but its grip and always with one of the hands acts the exchange of hands the player can only remain with a ball for 3 seconds.
