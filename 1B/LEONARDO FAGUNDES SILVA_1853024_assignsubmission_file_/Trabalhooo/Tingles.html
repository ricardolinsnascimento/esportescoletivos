The Main Tactical Systems of Volleyball

On this page, we will see the main tactical systems of volleyball.

Check:

  • The 6x0 System
  • The 3x3 System
  • The 4x2 System
  • The 5x1 System

6x0 system

Features:

  • There are no specialized players in a role;

  • All players are doweers, lifters and attackers;

  • Normally the player of position 3 (center of the net) will be the lifter;

  • The other 5 players will be Doweres and Attackers;

  • Allows all to play in different positions.

3x3 system

Features:

  • The team has 3 lifters and 3 attackers in an interleaved manner;

  • It is important for athletes to get a match for the success of matches;

  • Good possibility of an efficient counterattack;

  • Makes it hard to throw nine meters.

4x2 system

Features:

  • 4 attackers and 2 lifters;

  • The lifters are positioned on the diagonals of the court;

  • Makes the game more offensive and dynamic;

  • There is no Libero;

  • The defense positions itself in the form of a "w" to receive the sacing.

5x1 system

Features:

  • There are 1 lifter 5 attackers (Tips, Central and Opposite);

  • Not suitable for novice teams;

  • Uses libero;

  • Increased variation of attack plays.
