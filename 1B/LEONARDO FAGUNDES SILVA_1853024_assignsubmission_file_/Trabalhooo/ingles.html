Sport Origin

Created in 1895, in the United States, by William George Morgan (1870-1942), volleyball's first name was “mintonette”. Morgan's upbringing, who was a physical education teacher, was influenced by a number of factors, such as: having a game in which the risk of injury was reduced; in physical terms, it didn't demand much from the participants; could be played indoors and simply to innovate your classes.

Where is volleyball most played??

Fun facts about volleyball

  • The volleyball game with the largest audience in history was between the men's teams of Brazil and the Soviet Union, held on July 26, 1983. The Grand Volleyball Challenge was played on a court set up on the lawn of the Maracanã stadium (Rio de Janeiro) and had 95,000 spectators. Brazil won the match 3 sets to 1.
  • The longest volleyball match in history took place in the final of the Montreal Olympic Games (1976). The game between the Soviet Union and Poland lasted 4 hours and 36 minutes. The Polish team took the gold in this true sports marathon.
  • The Brazilian men's volleyball team has already won five medals at the Olympic Games, two gold and three silver. The women's team also won four Olympic medals (two gold and two bronze).
  • Currently, the Brazilian volleyball team is considered one of the best in the world.

The Foundation of the Brazilian Volleyball Confederation (CBV)

In 1954 the Brazilian Volleyball Confederation was founded (CBV).Until today, the CBV is the entity responsible for the sport in the country and thanks to it, volleyball was consolidated in Brazil.

The first president of the CBV was the former player Denis Rupet Hathaway, between March 14, 1955 and February 15, 1957.

Thanks to the work developed by this entity, today Brazil is one of the best countries in the world in volleyball.

Volleyball teams in Brazil

In the men's category, the team is a three-time world champion (1992, in Barcelona; 2004, in Athens; and 2016, in Rio de Janeiro) and has already won 6 Olympic medals (3 gold and 3 silver).

In the women's category, the team is a two-time Olympic champion (2008, in Beijing; 2012, in London) and has already won 5 Olympic medals (3 gold and 2 bronze).
